Cyber Lab
Embedded Systems • Edge Computing • Experimental Research Platform

An open hardware laboratory for exploring embedded systems, edge computing, electronics, sensing, and software development through practical experimentation.

Overview

Cyber Lab is a modular research and development platform built around a high-performance desktop workstation that serves as an electronics bench, software development environment, and hardware integration platform.

Rather than focusing on a single application, Cyber Lab provides a flexible workspace for prototyping, benchmarking, testing, and validating ideas across embedded systems, IoT devices, sensors, networking, and computational workloads.

The lab is designed to evolve continuously, allowing new hardware and software projects to be integrated as capabilities expand.

Mission

Cyber Lab exists to bridge software development with hands-on engineering by providing a unified environment where ideas can move quickly from concept to prototype.

Projects range from embedded firmware and hardware integration to desktop applications, networking, data visualization, and experimental sensing systems.

Open Source Philosophy

Cyber Lab is intended as a place for experimentation, learning, and collaboration. Successes, failures, and iterations are all valuable parts of the engineering process, and documenting that process is a core goal of the project.

Looking Ahead

As the lab grows, future work may include additional automation, expanded sensor networks, higher-performance storage and networking, and more advanced data acquisition systems. New concepts—such as novel magnetic field measurements or other exploratory engineering ideas—would be investigated by building prototypes, collecting measurements, and comparing results with established scientific understanding.

Project Motto

"Build. Measure. Learn. Repeat."
